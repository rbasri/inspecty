For home buyers and sellers:

Before closing, it's generally recommended that a home buyer purchases an inspection from a certified inspector. The inspection should last a couple hours, and at the end the customer will receive a written report documenting any potential issues with the property. Specifically, the inspector will be on the lookout for things that could present necessary fixes both in the near future and longer term.

Ultimately, a home is one of the most significant personal purchases one can make, so the cost of an inspection is well worth protecting the investment. Additionally, if the inspector does find issues with the home, it can be used to negotiate closing price and save the buyer money.

The base inspection will cover the majority of what you need to close on a home. As part of the base price, the inspector will check the exterior, interior, property grounds, driveway, roof, garage, crawl space, plumbing, and electricity for anything that may present an issue now or in the future.

While not required, we recommend add-ons to protect the investment in your home and guarantee a safe living space. Mold and radon, for example, can both present hazards to the occupant and lower the fair market value of the home if their presence is detected. Likewise, termites can be tremendously destructive to a home, and present significant costs if you unknowingly purchase a home with an infestation.

Finally, well flow testing and pool inspections are also available as simple and convenient ways to make sure you are really buying your perfect home.
